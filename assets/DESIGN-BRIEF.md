# Untether — visual identity design brief

A complete brief for designing the logo/branding for **Untether**. Hand to a design agent that can
rasterize (e.g. Claude Design).

## 1. What Untether is (the soul of the mark)

Untether is an open-source tool/skill that **reverse-engineers a Bluetooth/BLE consumer gadget that's
locked to its vendor phone app, and frees it** — it decodes the device's radio protocol and rebuilds
it as a **local, cloud-free Home Assistant integration**. You own the device, but only its app can
talk to it; Untether cuts that leash so it runs locally, forever, no cloud.

The emotional core is **liberation — cutting the tether**. Secondary themes from the actual work:
decoding the radio (Bluetooth advertisements, byte-by-byte "golden frames"), decompiling the app,
"static = the spec, dynamic = the truth," a craft practiced by hand, a self-improving library.

Tone: **authorized, defensive, pro-interoperability hacker** — liberation/unlock/decode energy, NOT
exploit/breach/malware energy. Confident, a little edgy, earned-not-corporate.

## 2. Audience & where it lives

Security researchers, hackers, and developers. The mark will appear as: a **GitHub repo header /
social preview**, a **README top**, **DEF CON-style slides**, **laptop stickers**, and a small
**skill/CLI avatar**. So it must work big *and* tiny, on dark and (occasionally) light backgrounds.

## 3. The big idea (one concept, executed well)

**A Bluetooth glyph breaking free of its tether.** The classic Bluetooth rune, with a **chain
link / leash snapping** (or a severed dashed line), and the freed device emitting **radio
advertisement arcs**. Optionally thread in a **byte row** — `[ 02 01 06 … ]` — with one byte lit in
an accent color: the "golden frame," the load-bearing artifact of the decode. Medium complexity: a
cool, memorable **emblem/badge**, not minimalist-corporate and not an over-detailed illustration.

Alternative angles the designer may explore: an open padlock-shackle drawn as a Bluetooth bond; the
word "untether" with the tether visually snapped between **un** | **tether**; a device silhouette
escaping a dotted "cloud" outline.

## 4. Aesthetic & references

DEF CON badge art · phrack/2600 zine energy · sharp open-source tool logos · terminal/CRT ·
restrained cyberpunk. Subtle scanline/glitch texture welcome **if it stays legible**. Think
"sticker I'd be proud to slap on a laptop and put on a conference slide," not "SaaS startup."

## 5. Color palette (exact)

- **Background:** near-black `#0A0E12` (also provide a transparent-bg version).
- **Primary:** phosphor/terminal green `#39FF14` (or the softer `#00FF9C` — designer's call).
- **Accent (pick one, use sparingly):** electric magenta `#FF2E97` *or* cyan `#00E5FF`.
- **"Golden frame" highlight:** warm gold `#FFC53D` for the one lit byte (optional but on-theme).
- Keep it mostly mono-green on black with a single accent pop. High contrast.

## 6. Typography

Lowercase **`untether`** as the wordmark in a **monospace or techno/geometric** face (e.g. JetBrains
Mono, Space Mono, IBM Plex Mono vibe, or a custom techno sans). Tight, bold, confident. The wordmark
should read instantly; the emblem carries the cleverness.

## 7. Deliverables (raster, please) + specs

1. **Primary horizontal lockup** — emblem + `untether` wordmark + a tagline slot. ~2400×800 px, PNG,
   transparent **and** `#0A0E12`-bg versions.
2. **Square emblem / avatar** — just the icon, **1024×1024** and **512×512**, transparent + dark.
   Must stay legible at 64px (favicon).
3. **Badge/sticker variant** — a circular or **hexagon** DEF CON-style badge containing the emblem +
   wordmark, die-cut friendly. ~1024×1024.
4. **GitHub social preview** — **1280×640**, the lockup centered with breathing room on the dark bg.
5. (Optional) a **monochrome 1-color** version (green-only and black-only) for stickers/embroidery.

Provide source where possible (SVG) plus the rasterized PNGs.

## 8. Do / don't

- **Do:** keep it legible at favicon size; one accent color; high contrast; balanced negative space.
- **Don't:** skulls, crossbones, ski-mask "hacker," padlock-with-binary clichés overdone, lens flares,
  more than one accent color, gradients that muddy at small sizes, anything that reads as malware.

## 9. Taglines (pick/refine — design one in, leave the wordmark usable without it)

- **"Local control. No cloud. No leash."**
- "Free your devices from their apps."
- "Static is the spec. Dynamic is truth."

## 10. Mood words

liberation · phosphor · radio · decode · snap · signal · craft · terminal · sovereign · local.

---

*One-line summary for the designer:* a phosphor-green-on-black DEF CON badge where a Bluetooth glyph
snaps its chain and radiates free, with the lowercase wordmark **untether** and a lit "golden-frame"
byte — liberation, not exploitation.
